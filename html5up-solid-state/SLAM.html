Obstacle Course Traversal Using SLAM

Explore and map an unknown area with a TurtleBot3

In this project, we were given a random obstacle course to explore and navigate through within 5 minutes using a TurtleBot3. Attached to the TurtleBot3 was a LiDAR that gave a point cloud of objects it detected. By representing the map as a grid, we used the point cloud readings to generate a configuration space along with potential frontiers to explore. Then, with A* path planning and PD controllers, the TurtleBot3 traversed to those frontier locations and obtained more readings for an internal map.

Once the internal map of the entire course was created, the TurtleBot3 was relocated to a random spot on the course, and then given a target location to reach. To re-localize itself, we used a probablistic monte-carlo localization (AMCL). Overall, we completed the designated objective with the physical robot in about 2 mins (2.5 times faster than requested).

RVIZ view of TurtleBot3 and Algorithms

The pink dot represents the starting point of exploration. The black line represents the A* path. The Green dots are the point cloud readings from the LiDAR. The blue is the unexplored regions, with the dark blue being the frontier. Orange is the generated configuration-space.

Gazebo Simulation of Map and TurtleBot3

To speed up testing, we simulated the TurtleBot3 and a potential obstacle course in Gazebo. This allowed us to tune our A* path planning heuristic, and determine if our frontier recognition algorithm is functional without needing to use physical bot or obstacle course.
